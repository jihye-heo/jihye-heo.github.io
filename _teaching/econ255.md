## Overview

These labs are designed to complement the theoretical material covered in class by emphasizing **hands-on empirical work** in Stata.

The sequence progresses from basic data handling to more advanced econometric methods:

- Introduction to Stata interface, workflows, and reproducibility  
- OLS estimation and interpretation  
- Data manipulation: merging, reshaping, and cleaning  
- Nonlinear models and interaction terms  
- Panel data methods, difference-in-differences, and fixed effects  
- Endogeneity and instrumental variables :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

Each lab session includes guided tasks, while assignments require independent application of the same techniques.

---

## Data Sources

The labs use a combination of datasets to expose students to both standard and real-world empirical settings:

- **Wooldridge textbook datasets** (e.g., wage, housing, loan application data) for canonical econometric examples :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  
- **Custom simulated datasets** created for this course to clearly illustrate specific econometric concepts :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  
- **Open-source data from the World Bank API**, where students directly download and construct country-year datasets within Stata (e.g., GDP, trade, inflation, and education indicators) :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

This combination allows students to replicate textbook results, understand underlying mechanisms in controlled settings, and work with real-world data.
